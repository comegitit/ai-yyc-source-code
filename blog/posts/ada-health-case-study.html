Human-Centred AI: A Medical Chatbot Example

January 2026 · Human Centred AI · Coursework, SAIT, Case Study · 2 Minute Read

In the transition toward AI-driven healthcare, success isn't just about algorithmic accuracy; it's about Human-Centred Design (HCD). For one of my "Human Centred AI" projects at SAIT, I analyzed the Ada Health Chatbot, a system that demonstrates how to bridge the gap between complex medical data and patient empathy.

The Technical Challenge: The Semantic Gap

A primary challenge for developers is the "Semantic Gap"—translating a user's subjective description (e.g., "my head is pounding") into structured clinical data. Ada uses advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP) to simulate a physician's history-taking process, making the interaction intuitive rather than clinical.

Strategic Features

Privacy by Design

To protect sensitive health data, user identities are stored separately from medical records.

Continuous Learning

The system employs a feedback loop where physician oversight refines the AI's probabilistic reasoning.

Accessibility

Ada adheres to WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards, ensuring the AI remains usable for individuals with motor or visual disabilities.

Key Takeaway: Ethical Guardrails

Innovation requires managing Automation Bias. During my research, interviews with medical professionals highlighted the "professional gut feeling" that algorithms cannot replicate. Furthermore, "Substitution Risk" remains a concern—where users might skip necessary professional care due to over-reliance on the app.

The Bottom Line

For AI to be sustainable, we must move toward "Human-in-the-Loop" designs. My analysis of Ada Health confirms that transparency and explainability are not just nice-to-haves, they are the foundation of user trust in high-profile environments.
